00  |  CV Foundation 
Universal principles that apply to every track. Internalise these before reading your specific path. 
 
Structure: The 9 Core Sections 
•​ Header - Full name, email, phone, 
LinkedIn, city 
•​ Professional Summary - 2-3 sentences, 
tailored to the role 
•​ Technical Skills - Grouped by category, 
honest levels only 
•​ Professional Experience - Most recent 
first 
•​ Projects - Links to deployed work 
whenever possible 
•​ Education - Degree, institution, GPA, awards 
•​ Certifications - Relevant 
technical/methodological certs 
•​ Additional - Languages, conferences, 
open-source contributions 
•​ Soft Skills - Brief mention; let experience 
sections prove them 
 
The Golden Formula 
 
Achieved X, as measured by Y, by doing Z 
Every bullet point in your Experience and Projects sections should follow this structure. Always add data 
- even rough numbers beat no numbers. 
 
Example (weak) 
Optimised backend API using Node.js. 
Example (strong) 
Optimised Node.js backend API, reducing system response time by 
40%. 
 
Top Misconceptions to Drop 
1. 
Extracurriculars > 
Experience 
Recruiters prioritise proven, real-world work over club activities 
or blood-donation records. 
 
2. 
Big Tech vs. Startup 
pedigree 
It does not matter where you worked - it matters how clearly 
you articulate what you achieved there. 
 
3. 
Hackathon awards = 
Work experience 
HR often discounts hackathon wins as unrealistic. They help, 
but never substitute for real project/internship experience. 
 


=== PAGE 3 ===
4. 
Listing skills is enough 
Recruiters can't verify a listed skill. Demonstrate it through 
achievements in your Experience or Projects sections. 
 
5. 
One CV for all roles 
Tailor per JD. If the company's stack is Java/Spring Boot, 
mention those terms explicitly - ATS filters aggressively. 
 
Formatting Rules 
•​ 1 page maximum for Intern / Junior / Fresher. Page 2 only from Senior level upward. 
•​ Always export as PDF (text-selectable, not scanned image). File name: Role - Your Name. 
•​ Use Harvard / clean single-column layout (e.g. UNC Computer Science template). 
•​ No unexplained employment gaps. No profile photo unless the JD explicitly requests one. 
•​ Contact info: phone, work email, LinkedIn, city only - keep it under one line. 
 
Action Verb Bank (from Checklist) 
Start every bullet with a strong verb. Group by intent: 
 
 
Leadership 
Spearheaded · Orchestrated · Delegated · Prioritised · Consolidated · Supervised 
 
 
Communication 
Liaised · Negotiated · Facilitated · Presented · Recruited · Synthesised 
 
 
Technical 
Architected · Optimised · Automated · Engineered · Streamlined · Deployed 
 
 
Research / Analysis 
Extracted · Diagnosed · Modelled · Investigated · Formulated · Systematised 
 
 
Quantitative 
Forecasted · Budgeted · Maximised · Minimised · Projected · Allocated

01  |  Product Management (PM) 
PM sits at the intersection of UX, Business, and Technology. Your CV must prove you can lead without 
authority, speak all three languages, and drive outcomes - not just ship features. 
 
Core Mindset 
1. 
Outcomes over Outputs 
Never say 'launched feature X'. Say 'launched feature X, 
increasing weekly active users by 15%'. Tie every action to a 
business metric. 
 
2. 
Cover the full product 
lifecycle 
Show you can move from Discovery → Strategy → Execution 
→ Go-to-Market → Iteration. One-phase PMs are a red flag. 
 
3. 
Cross-functional 
leadership is mandatory 
Name the stakeholders. 'Coordinated 10-person Engineering 
team, 2 Product Designers, and Marketing to ship campaign on 
time' beats 'worked with multiple teams'. 
 
4. 
Quantify with PM core 
metrics 
DAU/MAU, Retention Rate, Churn Rate, Conversion Rate, 
Revenue. Pick the metric that matters most for your product 
and tie results to it. 
 
5. 
Attach a Portfolio 
A Product Teardown (UX/user-flow analysis of any app) or a 
self-written Sample PRD proves Product Sense far more than a 
bullet point. 
 
6. 
Use strong leadership 
verbs 
Spearheaded · Aligned · Facilitated · Prioritised · Negotiated - 
these signal authority without the title. 
 
7. 
For non-tech 
backgrounds 
Show foundational tech knowledge (basic CS concepts, SQL, 
API awareness). Many companies now expect PM candidates 
to speak tech fluently. 
 
By Level 
  FRESHER / JUNIOR  (0 – 2 years)   
•​ Focus on PRD writing, user flow diagrams, and User Story decomposition. 


=== PAGE 5 ===
•​ Highlight hands-on execution: backlog management, sprint facilitation in clubs, student orgs, or 
community projects. 
•​ Tools that win: Figma (wireframes), Jira / Trello / Notion (task management), SQL + PowerBI (data 
independence). 
•​ Metrics to cite: Sprint completion rate, feature adoption in week 1, number of User Stories delivered. 
 
  MID-LEVEL  (2 – 5 years)   
•​ Show ownership of a full feature lifecycle end-to-end. 
•​ Highlight A/B Testing: hypothesis → experiment → statistical decision. 
•​ Tools: Amplitude / Mixpanel / GA4 (analytics), Productboard / Aha! (roadmapping), Optimizely / 
Firebase (A/B). 
•​ Metrics: DAU/MAU, Session Length, 7-day / 30-day Retention, Funnel Conversion Rate. 
 
  SENIOR / LEAD  (5+ years)   
•​ Lead with Business Impact: market share expansion, new market entry, monetisation optimisation. 
•​ Show framework-building and mentorship of junior PMs. 
•​ Tools: Tableau / Looker (data governance), OKR tracking tools. 
•​ Metrics: MRR/ARR, LTV, CAC, GMV, NPS, CSAT, team size managed, budget owned. 
 
ATS KEYWORDS 
Product Lifecycle · Roadmap · Product Backlog · User Story · Agile/Scrum · A/B Testing · Go-to-Market 
(GTM) · Cross-functional Leadership · Data-driven · User Research 
 


=== PAGE 6 ===
02  |  Business Analysis (BA) 
BA is the translator between Business and Technology. Your CV must show you are a problem-solver and 
storyteller - not a note-taker. Demonstrate a clear logical thread: problem identified → requirements 
gathered → tech solution proposed → outcome delivered. 
 
Core Mindset 
1. 
Traceability is everything 
Show a logical thread: problem → requirements elicitation → 
proposed tech solution → final outcome. This proves you think 
end-to-end, not task-by-task. 
 
2. 
Convert documents into 
impact 
Don't say 'wrote BRD/SRS'. Say 'Completed BRD/SRS for CRM 
system, reducing Dev team's coding time by 15% through 
clearer logic documentation'. 
 
3. 
Stakeholder Management 
is a skill 
Name who you worked with: C-level, Marketing, Dev, QA. 
Describe how you resolved conflicting requirements - this 
differentiates senior thinkers. 
 
4. 
Precision in flow 
modelling 
Flowcharts, Use Cases, User Stories: show care and accuracy. 
These artefacts are your primary deliverables - treat them as 
such. 
 
5. 
Tech literacy is now 
expected 
Postman/Swagger (API testing), SQL, basic understanding of 
data flows and system architecture. Being able to talk to Devs 
as a peer is a significant differentiator. 
 
6. 
Certifications signal 
commitment 
ECBA (Fresher), CCBA (Mid), CBAP (Senior) from IIBA. PSM I / 
CSM for Agile roles. Include if you have them. 
 
By Level 
  FRESHER / JUNIOR  (0 – 2 years)   
•​ Use academic or course projects if no work experience - state which elicitation techniques you 
applied: Interview, Survey, Document Analysis. 
•​ Emphasise accuracy in flow diagrams (Draw.io, Lucidchart, Visio) and written Use Cases / User Stories. 
•​ Tools: Jira/Trello (task), Balsamiq/Figma (wireframes), Excel (Pivot Table, VLOOKUP), basic SQL. 


=== PAGE 7 ===
•​ Metrics: number of Use Cases/User Stories written, logic errors caught in UAT, on-time document 
delivery rate. 
 
  MID-LEVEL  (2 – 5 years)   
•​ Show methodology: Agile/Scrum vs Waterfall and your specific role in ceremonies (Sprint Planning, 
Backlog Refinement). 
•​ Demonstrate system analysis: data flows, API understanding, architecture awareness - proof you can 
talk to Devs without a translator. 
•​ Tools: BPMN 2.0 (critical for process modelling), Confluence/SharePoint, Postman/Swagger, 
PowerBI/Tableau. 
•​ Metrics: Requirement Churn Rate (lower = better), % reduction in manual processing time, 
time-to-market improvement. 
 
  SENIOR / LEAD  (5+ years)   
•​ Lead with Domain Knowledge depth: Fintech, E-commerce, Healthcare, ERP - name your vertical. 
•​ Show 'Push-back' capability: examples of using data/logic to reject low-ROI feature requests from 
stakeholders. 
•​ Tools: SAP/Oracle/Odoo (ERP), Salesforce/MS Dynamics (CRM), Enterprise Architect tools. 
•​ Metrics: financial impact ($-saved or %-revenue-added), User Adoption Rate of launched system, 
portfolio project success rate (on-time, on-budget). 
 
ATS KEYWORDS 
BRD · SRS · FRS · UAT · Stakeholder Management · Requirements Gathering · As-Is & To-Be Process · Gap 
Analysis · Backlog Grooming · BPMN · Agile/Scrum 
 


=== PAGE 8 ===
03  |  Data Analytics (DA) 
Data Analysts turn raw, messy numbers into decisions. Your CV must prove the formula: Tool X → Dataset Y 
→ Insight Z → Business Action W. Automation mindset and clear business impact are what separate 
average CVs from standout ones. 
 
Core Mindset 
1. 
Embrace the mess 
80% of a DA's time is data cleaning. Show you are not afraid of 
raw, incomplete, inconsistent datasets - missing values, 
duplicates, and all. 
 
2. 
Business Impact is the 
only metric 
A beautiful dashboard is worthless if nobody uses it to make a 
decision. Every project bullet must end with a business action 
or outcome. 
 
3. 
Automation > Manual 
If you built a script or pipeline that auto-generates a weekly 
report, say how many manual hours it saves. Hiring managers 
love this. 
 
4. 
Quantify everything 
Include dataset size (rows/GB), number of dashboards built, % 
data errors removed, hours saved, % uplift in KPIs. 
 
5. 
SQL + PowerBI is your 
base combo 
For Fresher/Junior level, this pairing is the standard 'weapon' 
for passing CV screens. Do not skip either. 
 
6. 
DA needs soft skills too 
Unlike SWE, DAs communicate findings to non-technical 
stakeholders daily. Add: Cross-functional Collaboration, Data 
Storytelling, Stakeholder Management. 
 
By Level 
  FRESHER / JUNIOR  (0 – 2 years)   
•​ Personal projects from Kaggle or public datasets are perfectly valid. Host on GitHub with clear 
README. Link to live dashboards if possible. 
•​ Document your pipeline: Extract → Clean → Analyse → Visualise. Show all four stages. 
•​ Tools: SQL (CTEs, Window Functions), PowerBI or Tableau, Excel (Pivot Table, Power Query). 
•​ Metrics: dataset size processed (e.g. 500,000+ rows), number of dashboards built and their purpose. 
 


=== PAGE 9 ===
 
Weak vs. Strong - Junior Example 
Weak: 'Used PowerBI to make weekly sales charts.'  →  Strong: 'Built automated PowerBI sales 
dashboard visualising 100,000+ transactions, eliminating 4 hours of weekly manual reporting.' 
 
  MID-LEVEL  (2 – 5 years)   
•​ Shift from descriptive (What happened?) to diagnostic (Why did it happen?) analysis, and begin 
advising other departments. 
•​ Showcase A/B Testing: experiment design, sample splitting, statistical hypothesis testing to validate 
conclusions. 
•​ Tools: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib), GA4 / Mixpanel / Amplitude, Google BigQuery / Snowflake. 
•​ Metrics: campaign ROI improvement (e.g. +20%), CAC reduction (e.g. -15%), hours of manual work 
automated per week. 
 
  SENIOR / LEAD  (5+ years)   
•​ You own the organisation's Data Culture. Show Data Architecture and Data Governance work. 
•​ Demonstrate direct work with C-Level to set company-wide OKRs/KPIs. 
•​ Tools: dbt, Apache Airflow (pipeline scheduling), AWS Redshift / GCP / Azure (Cloud DW ecosystems), 
Tableau / Looker. 
•​ Metrics: cross-sell/up-sell revenue unlocked from data insights, % speed improvement in data 
discovery, team size led. 
 
ATS KEYWORDS 
Data Cleansing · Data Visualisation · ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) · A/B Testing · Predictive Modelling · 
Statistical Analysis · Dashboard · Cohort Analysis · Funnel Analysis · Data Storytelling · Stakeholder 
Management

04  |  Software Engineering (SWE) 
SWE CVs live and die on demonstrated technical depth. Recruiters look for clean code, scalability thinking, 
and performance results. Show, don't tell: link to GitHub, cite benchmark numbers, prove your code 
survives production. 
 
Core Mindset 
1. 
Show, Don't Tell 
Never write 'Proficient in React'. Write 'Built SPA with React + 
Redux, achieved Lighthouse score 95+'. GitHub links are 
non-negotiable. 
 
2. 
Quantify optimisation 
Good engineers solve resource problems. Always measure: 
response time (ms), memory usage, CPU load, cost savings. 
Numbers prove impact. 
 
3. 
Testing = Production 
mindset 
Writing Unit Tests, Integration Tests, and understanding CI/CD 
pipelines signals you build maintainable software - not just 
code that 'runs on my machine'. 
 
4. 
Group your Tech Stack 
clearly 
Avoid a long undifferentiated list. Use four categories: 
Languages · Frontend/Backend Frameworks · Databases · 
Cloud & DevOps.  
Cleaner = easier to scan. 
 
5. 
SWE proves value through 
technical metrics 
Unlike PM/BA, your core proof points are: latency, throughput, 
uptime %, code coverage %, cost reduction - not business 
revenue. 
 
By Level 
  FRESHER / JUNIOR  (0 – 2 years)   
•​ GitHub repos must have a clean README with setup instructions and architecture overview. 
•​ Show Git discipline: branching, merge, rebase - not just 'I pushed to main'. 
•​ Emphasise what you learned from fixing bugs and reviewing code - growth mindset signals. 
•​ Tools: Core language + framework you truly understand (JS/React, Java/Spring, Python/Django), SQL 
basics, Git/GitHub. 
•​ Metrics: code coverage % (e.g. 80% with Jest/JUnit), bug reduction rate, LeetCode / HackerRank 
ranking (big plus for Freshers). 
 


=== PAGE 11 ===
  MID-LEVEL  (2 – 5 years)   
•​ Shift from feature-builder to system-optimiser. How did you address Technical Debt? 
•​ Show Agile/Scrum teamwork and early mentoring of Juniors. 
•​ Tools: Redis/Memcached (caching), Docker, AWS/GCP/Azure basics, CI/CD (GitHub Actions, Jenkins), 
Microservices, RESTful API design. 
•​ Metrics: API latency reduction (e.g. 500ms → 100ms), SQL optimisation reducing DB CPU by 30%, 
CCU (concurrent users) supported. 
 
  SENIOR / LEAD / PRINCIPAL  (5+ years)   
•​ You are the System Architect: own Tech Stack decisions, High Availability, and engineering culture. 
•​ Focus on System Design: Distributed Systems, Event-driven Architecture, Data Consistency at scale. 
•​ Show how technical decisions connect to business goals (e.g. 'chose X technology, saving $Y/month 
in server costs'). 
•​ Tools: Kubernetes, Kafka/RabbitMQ, ElasticSearch, Prometheus/Grafana/Datadog (observability). 
•​ Metrics: 99.99% uptime, millions of transactions/day (QPS), cloud cost reduction ($X,000/month), 
CI/CD cycle time reduction. 
 
ATS KEYWORDS  
Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) · REST API · Microservices · CI/CD · Unit Testing · Design Patterns · 
Scalability · Refactoring · Database Optimisation · Clean Code 
 


=== PAGE 12 ===
05  |  AI / Machine Learning (AI/ML) 
AI/ML CVs must prove you can ship production-ready models - not just hit accuracy scores in a notebook. 
Business ROI from deployed AI is the ultimate differentiator. 
 
Core Mindset 
1. 
Production-Ready > High 
Accuracy 
A 90%-accurate model running on a real server beats a 
99%-accurate model stuck in a Jupyter notebook. Always 
highlight 'deployed' work. 
 
2. 
Translate AI into Business Every model result must connect to business impact. 
'Recommendation model increased conversion rate by X%'. 
'Chatbot saved Y hours/day for support team'. 
 
3. 
Data quality is 
foundational 
Show awareness of data labelling, cleaning, and handling class 
imbalance. 80% of ML work is data - embrace it. 
 
4. 
Escape the Notebook trap Wrap your model in a FastAPI/Flask endpoint. Package with 
Docker. Deploy a Streamlit/Gradio demo. This alone separates 
you from 80% of applicants. 
 
5. 
LLMs and RAG are now 
table stakes for Mid+ 
Experience integrating Large Language Models and 
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a fast-growing 
expectation at Mid-level and above. 
 
6. 
Use the right model 
metrics 
F1-Score, Precision, Recall, AUC-ROC - never cite Accuracy 
alone, especially on imbalanced datasets. It signals model 
evaluation maturity. 
 
7. 
Publications = instant 
credibility 
Papers at CVPR, NeurIPS, ACL? Put them at the top of your CV. 
Even for applied roles, ability to read and re-implement SOTA 
research is a survival skill. 
 
By Level 
  FRESHER / JUNIOR  (0 – 2 years)   
•​ Choose practical project topics: text classification, product defect detection - not overused Titanic / Iris 
datasets. 


=== PAGE 13 ===
•​ Wrap model in API (FastAPI/Flask) + Docker. Demo with Streamlit or Gradio. Link everything. 
•​ Tools: PyTorch or TensorFlow/Keras, Scikit-learn, Pandas/NumPy, FastAPI/Flask, Docker. 
•​ Metrics: F1/Precision/Recall/AUC-ROC, inference speed (e.g. '<200ms per image'), dataset size 
processed. 
 
  MID-LEVEL  (2 – 5 years)   
•​ MLOps is your core keyword. Show management of the full model lifecycle: training → deployment → 
monitoring → retraining. 
•​ Highlight Model Drift handling and automated retraining pipelines. 
•​ Show LLM integration and RAG implementation experience. 
•​ Tools: MLflow / W&B / DVC (lifecycle), Kubernetes + AWS SageMaker / GCP Vertex AI (infra), Pinecone 
/ Milvus / ChromaDB (vector DB). 
•​ Metrics: dataset scale (e.g. 100GB trained), model compression (e.g. 50% RAM reduction via 
quantisation), business ROI (e.g. 10,000 tickets auto-classified/day at 92% accuracy). 
 
  SENIOR / PRINCIPAL  (5+ years)   
•​ You design the full AI Architecture and make the build/buy/fine-tune decision for the org. 
•​ Prove cost-awareness: GPU/cloud cost optimisation at scale is the #1 concern at this level. 
•​ Show distributed training experience across multiple GPUs/nodes. 
•​ Tools: Triton Inference Server, TensorRT, vLLM (LLM serving), Ray / DeepSpeed / Horovod (distributed 
training). 
•​ Metrics: throughput improvement (e.g. 3x), GPU/cloud cost savings (e.g. $X0,000/month), internal AI 
platform serving 5+ product teams. 
 
ATS KEYWORDS 
MLOps · LLM · Prompt Engineering · RAG · Fine-tuning · Computer Vision · NLP · Recommendation 
Systems · Model Deployment · Docker · Kubernetes · PyTorch · Feature Engineering
